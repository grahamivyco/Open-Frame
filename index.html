Park n Go

Reimagining the event parking experience — a mobile app that lets event-goers reserve their spot in advance, before the stress ever starts.

Role

Sole prototyper

Scope

Research → UI · testing

Timeline

3-week sprint

Tools

Figma · FigJam · Miro

Background

Parking is the one part of a night out no one plans for.

Everyday parking already had plenty of apps, so we narrowed in on the gap no one had solved: event and concert parking — where fans plan every part of a show except where they'll actually park.

How might we take the most stressful part of attending an event and make it feel effortless?

Discover

A stronger signal than we expected.

A survey and four in-depth interviews pointed to the same pain point again and again — parking for events and concerts specifically. The numbers were too consistent to ignore.

100%struggle to find parking at an event
“

78%would pay to reserve in advance
“

I'd happily pay upfront just to skip the stress on the night.

66%spend 10–20 min hunting for a spot
“

No app shows me real-time availability before I leave home.

Define

Who we're designing for.

We framed a primary user and mapped how the parking journey actually feels — where the stress spikes, and where a good app could turn it around.

User persona
Sarah, 27Frequent concert-goer · Houston, TX

Goes to two or three live shows a month around Houston. She plans the outfit, the dinner, and the group — but never the parking, and it's the one thing that turns an exciting night into a stressful scramble.

Goals
  • Reserve a spot before leaving home
  • See price & walking distance up front
  • Arrive calm, not frazzled
Frustrations
  • Circling unfamiliar lots as the show starts
  • No idea what parking costs until she's there
  • Ending up parked far from the entrance

Design

Park n Go: seamless event parking.

A high-contrast dark UI with a signal-gold accent — prototyped and user-tested from mid- to high-fidelity. Move your cursor to tilt it; the screens cycle on their own.

Deliver

From a stressful scramble to a few taps.

Park n Go turns the most overlooked part of a night out into something effortless — reserve ahead, arrive easy. The three-week sprint reinforced how much a tightly-scoped problem pays off: by resisting the urge to solve all parking and committing to the event-night moment, every screen earned its place.

All work Responsive Redesign · Nonprofit

Hmong Cultural Center

Revitalizing a community's online identity — a responsive redesign for a Saint Paul cultural hub, built around accessibility, belonging, and a clear path to resources.

Role

Sole prototyper · research

Scope

Discovery, strategy & design

Timeline

3-week bootcamp
Overview

Designing for a real community hub.

Over a three-week UX bootcamp, my team partnered with the Hmong Cultural Center in Saint Paul — a hub offering everything from traditional embroidery to citizenship courses. We set out to revitalize its branding, streamline its information architecture, and improve access to resources for the people it serves.

The approach

The Double Diamond.

Four phases — Discover, Define, Develop, Deliver — from a wide-open problem space to a focused, shippable redesign for mobile and desktop.

01

Discover

A heuristic audit of the current site plus surveys and interviews. Because the center is unique, we analyzed sites serving similar markets — and listened to what a successful new-user experience needed to feel like.

02

Define

We met Mai Yer Xiong — a first-generation immigrant with limited English — and framed the problem: a more visual site with prominent iconography and easy access to English-language and citizenship classes.

03

Develop

The old sitemap buried the most important resources. We simplified the IA, mapped a fast user flow to language classes, and moved from paper to mid-fidelity wireframes with a vibrant, high-contrast UI kit.

04

Deliver

Mid- to high-fidelity in three days — a signup-focused hero, simplified color-coded navigation, and branded section banners, all consistent across mobile and desktop.

Listening first.

Built for signup, easy to navigate.

Reflections

Strong systems = success.

A robust design system — consistent color, typography, and layout — kept the site cohesive and genuinely usable for English-language learners, while weaving Hmong cultural elements into the identity and surfacing key offerings like ESL and citizenship classes.

Delivering a full redesign in three weeks demanded tight prioritization and agile teamwork — and we shipped a high-fidelity prototype for both mobile and desktop.

HelloSpoke

Simplifying enterprise communications through strategic product-marketing design — a scalable marketing website that turns a complex unified-communications platform into a clear, sales-ready story.

Role

Product & Web Design

Scope

Site, IA, design system

Timeline

2023 — 2024
hellospoke.com
Overview & challenge

A complex platform that wasn't landing.

HelloSpoke keeps property teams connected — from first call to completed request. But a broad, technical product had a website that didn't communicate its value, with overlapping features that made it hard for prospects to understand what was on offer.

  • Overlapping services, unclear messaging
  • Technical language overwhelming buyers
  • No clear funnel, no scalable system
hellospoke.com · before

The original site — dense and hard to parse.

The approach

Research to shipped site.

A full product-marketing workflow — understand the business, restructure the story, systematize the design, and build it responsive.

01

Discovery

Got up to speed on the communications industry, mapped customer personas, and reviewed competitors — surfacing the real pain points and the questions every visitor was trying to answer.

02

Information architecture

Restructured navigation so each product had a home and each journey had a clear route — Core Platform, Assist, Dialtone, Pricing, Resources, About — reducing cognitive load for non-technical buyers.

03

UX strategy

Anchored the whole site around the five questions a buyer asks — what it does, is it for me, why it's different, what it costs, how to start — and made each page answer one clearly.

04

Design system & build

Built a reusable system — type, color, buttons, cards, spacing — then shipped it responsive in Divi on WordPress, so new product pages could be assembled quickly and stay on-brand.

Email newsletters, Facebook templates, podcast branding, and presentation decks that all read as one organization.

Brand & Web Designer

Scope

Identity, website, UX

2024 — 2025

Heritage that was hard to reach.

Since 1972, the Guild has fostered excellence in needlework through classes, retreats, and a passionate community. But the old site made it hard to find what members care about most — classes, retreats, and membership — and the identity didn't reflect the warmth of the craft or invite newcomers in.

needleworkguildmn.org · before

The site before the refresh.

  • Make classes, retreats & membership easy to find
  • A warmer, more welcoming identity
  • Honor the Guild's 50-year heritage
  • Keep it simple for volunteers to maintain
